# 🗣️ Sales Objections — Ready Responses in FYS Brand Voice

This file contains the **6 most common objections** FYS sales reps encounter at bakeries, with ready-to-use responses in the official brand tone: direct, slightly sharp, no-nonsense.

---

## Objection 1 — "I've never heard of this brand."

**What the owner is really saying:** I don't trust the product because I don't know it.

**Sales rep response:**
> "That makes sense. FYS doesn't invest in billboards on your block. It's from the Heineken Group, three years on the market, and growing in exactly the places that gave it a chance. Want to see the label?"

**Why it works:** Validates the objection without backing down, anchors credibility in the Heineken Group, and invites the next step without pressure.

---

## Objection 2 — "My customers only ask for Coke or Guaraná."

**What the owner is really saying:** I don't want to risk money on a product that won't sell.

**Sales rep response:**
> "Sure, 80% do. But the other 20% leave without buying because there's no different option. FYS isn't here to replace Coke — it's here to convert the customer who was walking out empty-handed."

**Why it works:** Uses the owner's own data against the objection. FYS doesn't compete — it complements.

---

## Objection 3 — "I already have too many soft drinks in my fridge."

**What the owner is really saying:** I don't have space (or don't want to reorganize).

**Sales rep response:**
> "You don't need a whole shelf. Start with 6 units in the corner. If it doesn't sell in 2 weeks, we talk. But it will sell."

**Why it works:** Removes the risk barrier. Six cans is the lowest possible commitment in space and capital.

---

## Objection 4 — "I don't have money to test a new product right now."

**What the owner is really saying:** I don't want to spend money on something uncertain.

**Sales rep response:**
> "The entry order is small — one case of 12 units. Sell the 12, recover the investment, and you've already made margin to decide if you want to keep going. We're talking about less than [value] to test."

**Why it works:** Translates the risk into a small, concrete number. Puts the focus on the return, not the cost.

---

## Objection 5 — "Low-sugar drinks don't sell in bakeries."

**What the owner is really saying:** My customer base isn't that type.

**Sales rep response:**
> "That was the thinking in 2018. Today even gyms sell fried snacks. The bakery customer isn't just the 60-year-old anymore — there are moms, young people, people checking labels. FYS rides that wave."

**Why it works:** Challenges the premise with real cultural data. Updates the owner's view without offending them.

---

## Objection 6 — "Let me think about it and I'll get back to you."

**What the owner is really saying:** I don't want to decide right now (or don't want to say no to your face).

**Sales rep response (on the spot):**
> "Of course. Can I send you a WhatsApp tomorrow with the details? That way you have everything you need to decide with no rush."

**Follow-up message (next day):**
> "Hey [name]! It's me — I stopped by yesterday with FYS. Leaving my contact here in case you want to confirm the first order. Intro promo is good until Friday. No pressure — but if you want to lock in the test price, this week's the window."

**Why it works:** No pressure. Creates a next step with a soft deadline and removes the feeling of being pestered.

---

## Golden Rule for Sales Reps

Never use more than 3 sentences per response in the field. The bakery owner is always busy. The shorter it is, the more likely you are to be heard.
